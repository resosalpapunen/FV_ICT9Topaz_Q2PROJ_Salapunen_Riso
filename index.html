Breakfast - Poqui Poqui

Ingredients

  • 2 Eggplants
  • 4 Eggs
  • Onion (sliced)
  • Tomatoes (diced)
  • Oil
  • 1 Clove Garlic (minced)
  • Salt (pinch)
  • Pepper (pinch)

Recipe

Grill eggplants over a gas stove or hot coals until the skins are browned. Take off the heat, rinse well, remove the skin, and run water over it. Cut the meat coarsely. Heat the oil in a skillet over medium heat. Cook the garlic and onions until they are tender. Continue cooking after adding the tomatoes, mashing them with the back of the spoon until they are tender. Add the eggplant. To taste, add salt and pepper. Stir the beaten eggs into the mixture and cook until the eggs are barely set. Warm up and serve.

Lunch - Pinakbet

Ingredients

  • Pork Belly (Sliced)
  • Knorr Shrimp Cube (1pc)
  • Sitaw (Precut)
  • Kalabasa (Cubed)
  • Okra (12pcs)
  • Eggplant (Sliced)
  • Ampalaya (Sliced)
  • Kamote (Cubed)
  • Tomato (Cubed)
  • Ginger (Crushed)
  • Onion (Chopped)
  • Garlic (4clvs, Crushed)
  • Bagoong (2tsp)
  • 2 cups of water
  • cooking oil (3tbs)
  • Black pepper (1/4)

Recipe

In a pot, heat cooking oil. Add the garlic and onion and sauté. After adding the ginger, keep cooking until the onion becomes tender. Include lechon kawali. For one minute, sauté. Fill with water. Bring to a boil. Put in some Knorr Shrimp Cube. Cook for 20 minutes over medium heat with a lid on. Add the bagoong alamang and tomato. Stir. Put a lid on the pot. Cook for three minutes. Add the kamote and kalabasa to the pot. Cook for seven minutes. Add eggplant, ampalaya, okra, and sitaw. Stir. Cook, covered, for five minutes. Add the leftover lechon kawali and season with ground black pepper. Cook for a few minutes.

Dinner - Igado

Ingredients

  • Pork Tenderloin (1/2inch Strips)
  • Vinegar (1/2 cup)
  • Soy Sauce (1/4 cup)
  • Onion (thin slice)
  • Bay Leaves (2 pcs)
  • Peppercorns (cracked)
  • Garlic (minced)
  • Oil (1tbsp)
  • Red pepper (Julienne cut)
  • Water (1 cup)
  • Beef liver (1/2inch strips)
  • Green Peas
  • Salt (tbsp)

Recipe

Put the pork strips, bay leaves, peppercorns, onions, garlic, vinegar, and soy sauce in a bowl. Let it marinate for 15 to 20 minutes. Squeeze off any extra liquid after draining the meat from the marinade. Keep the aromatics and marinade for later. Heat the oil in a pan over medium-high heat. Cook for about 30 seconds after adding the bell peppers. Take out and put aside. Add the pork to the pan and heat, stirring now and again, until the color changes. Add the bay leaves (from marinating the pork), onions, garlic, and peppercorns. Cook until the pork is tender and has a light brown. Bring to a boil, uncovered, without stirring, for approximately 4 to 5 minutes after adding the reserved marinade. Bring to a boil after adding water. Simmer over low heat, covered, for 25 to 30 minutes, or until the pork is It is less liquid and delicate. Stir gently to mix in the liver, then heat for another 4 to 5 minutes or until the sauce has thickened and the liver is cooked through. Cook the green peas for two to three minutes, or until they are well heated. After adding the bell peppers, sauté for a further one to two minutes, or until they are crisp but still soft. Add salt to taste. Warm up and serve.

Dessert - Tambo Tambo

Ingredients

  • Glutinous Rice Flour (1 cup)
  • Water (2 1/2 cup)
  • Coconut Milk (3 cans)
  • Kamote (Cubed)
  • Bananas (Cubed)
  • Sago (1 cup)
  • Jackfruit strips (1 cup)
  • Coconut cream (1 cup)
  • Sugar (1 cup)

Recipe

Glutinous rice flour and ½ cup water should be combined in a small bowl to form a soft dough. Add extra flour if the dough is too sticky, and more water if it is too crumbly. Shape the dough into marble-sized, smooth balls with the palms of your hands. On a shallow plate or baking sheet, arrange the shaped dough balls in a single layer. Cover with a cloth until you're ready to use them. Put the remaining two cups of water and coconut milk in a large pot over medium heat. Bring to a simmer. Cook the camote for three to five minutes, or until it is nearly soft. Cook the jackfruit, bananas, and sago for three to five minutes. Cook the sticky rice balls for until they begin to float on top, which should take three to five minutes. Stir in the sugar until it dissolves. After adding the coconut cream, keep cooking until the camote and bananas are soft and the sauce has the consistency you want. Serve cold or warm.
